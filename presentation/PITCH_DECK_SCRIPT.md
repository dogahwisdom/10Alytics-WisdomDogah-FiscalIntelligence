# Complete Pitch Script: Natural, Conversational Version

## Opening (35 seconds)

"Good [morning/afternoon]. I want to start with a question: How many of you are working toward the Sustainable Development Goals?

[Pause for response]

I'm going to show you something that accelerates SDG achievement. We analyzed $69 billion in fiscal data across 14 African countries over 65 years. And we found something surprising: the tools we use to make billion-dollar decisions that affect millions of people are still from the 1960s.

But here's the good news: we built something better. Something that directly advances 8 Sustainable Development Goals. And I'm going to show you how it works."

---

## The Problem (45 seconds)

"Let me paint a picture. A government needs to plan next year's budget. An analyst—let's call her Sarah—spends four weeks gathering data. Another two weeks cleaning it. By week six, she's finally running analysis. But by then, the information is already outdated.

Meanwhile, patterns are hiding in the data. Opportunities are being missed. And Sarah? She's spending 70% of her time cleaning data instead of finding insights.

We looked at fiscal data from 14 African countries. 23,784 data points. 65 years of history. And we found that most organizations are really good at tracking inflation—2,879 records. But revenue and spending? Only 1,752 records.

It's like having a speedometer but no fuel gauge. You'll crash eventually."

---

## Why This Matters (40 seconds)

"This isn't just about data. It's about decisions that affect millions of people.

For governments: Every dollar you allocate without complete data is a gamble. We found that countries with better data systems—like South Africa, which tracks 20 different indicators—make better decisions.

For banks: You're making lending decisions based on incomplete information. We can show you patterns that predict fiscal health months in advance.

For institutions: Your analysts are data janitors instead of strategists. That's like paying a chef to wash dishes.

The cost? Millions in wasted resources. The opportunity? Transform how you make decisions."

## What We Discovered (50 seconds)

We looked at 65 years of fiscal data—from when your grandparents were making these decisions to today. Here's what jumped out:

Rwanda has the highest fiscal activity—$42 billion. But they have fewer data points than other countries. That means every decision has bigger impact. Imagine if they had better data to guide those decisions.

South Africa is the gold standard. They track 20 different indicators—twice as many as most countries. They're not just collecting data; they're using it.

And this is the big one: fiscal activity has grown 9 times over 65 years. Nine times. But are we making decisions 9 times better? No. We're using the same old methods."

You feed it your fiscal data. It automatically cleans it—no more spending weeks fixing errors. It finds patterns you'd never see—like how inflation in one country predicts spending in another. It spots problems before they become crises.

And here's the part executives love: it doesn't just tell you what happened. It predicts what will happen. And it recommends what you should do.

All in minutes, not weeks."

## DELIVERY NOTES

### Timing
- **Total**: 10-12 minutes
- **Practice with timer**: Know which parts to emphasize or skip
- **Leave 2-3 minutes** for questions

### Emphasis Points
- **Pause after big numbers**: "$69 billion", "9 times", "2 hours"
- **Slow down for key insights**: The four discoveries
- **Speed up through technical details**: Keep it high-level

### Body Language
- **Make eye contact**: Don't just read the slides
- **Use gestures**: Point to visuals, use hands to emphasize
- **Move naturally**: Don't hide behind podium
- **Show confidence**: You've done the work, own it

### Voice
- **Vary your pace**: Fast for excitement, slow for impact
- **Vary your volume**: Whisper for emphasis, normal for facts
- **Use pauses**: Let important points sink in
- **Sound natural**: Like you're explaining to a friend

### Handling Questions
- **Listen completely**: Don't interrupt
- **Repeat the question**: So everyone hears it
- **Be honest**: If you don't know, say so
- **Connect to your story**: Every answer reinforces your message
