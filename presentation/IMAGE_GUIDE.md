# Complete Image Guide for Pitch Deck

## General Image Guidelines

### Style
- **Professional but approachable** - Not too corporate, not too casual
- **Modern and clean** - Avoid dated or cluttered visuals
- **Diverse representation** - Show people from different backgrounds
- **High resolution** - Minimum 1920x1080 for presentations
- **Consistent color scheme** - Use your brand colors throughout

### Where to Find Images
1. **Unsplash.com** - Free, high-quality photos (best for people/scenes)
2. **Pexels.com** - Free stock photos (great variety)
3. **Freepik.com** - Icons and illustrations (some free, some paid)
4. **Flaticon.com** - Free icons (perfect for infographics)
5. **Your own screenshots** - Use actual dashboards/charts from your analysis

### Image Placement Rules
- **One main image per slide** - Don't clutter
- **Text overlay on images** - Use semi-transparent boxes for readability
- **Consistent positioning** - Keep images in same general area
- **White space** - Don't fill every inch

## QUICK REFERENCE: IMAGE CHECKLIST

### For Each Slide:
- [ ] High resolution (1920x1080 minimum)
- [ ] Professional quality
- [ ] Consistent style
- [ ] Properly licensed (free or purchased)
- [ ] Text readable over image (use overlay if needed)
- [ ] Matches slide content
- [ ] Diverse representation (if showing people)

### Image Sources Quick Links:
- **Unsplash**: unsplash.com (free, no attribution required)
- **Pexels**: pexels.com (free, no attribution required)
- **Flaticon**: flaticon.com (free icons with attribution)
- **Freepik**: freepik.com (some free, some paid)
- **Your Charts**: Use screenshots from `reports/plots/`

### Pro Tips:
1. **Use your actual data visualizations** for Slide 6 - this is your proof!
2. **Screenshot your dashboard** (`trend_dashboard.html`) - it's impressive
3. **Create simple infographics** for numbers - Canva has great templates
4. **Keep it consistent** - same style throughout
5. **Test readability** - can you read text over images?
6. **Have backups** - download multiple options, pick the best

---

*Remember: Images should support your story, not distract from it. When in doubt, simpler is better.*
